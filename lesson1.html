LESSON ONE
The Sounds of French: the international phonetic alphabet
and French "phonemes" (sounds).

In this lesson we will learn to pronounce the French phonemes
and identify their symbols from the International Phonetic Alphabet.

There are 35 French phonemes altogether: 15 vowels, 17 consonants, and 3 semi-vowels.

Listen to and repeat the following vowel sounds:

i y u e ø o ɛ œ ɔ a ɑ ɛ̃ œ̃ ã ɔ̃

Listen to and repeat the following semi-vowels:

j ɥ w

Listen to and repeat the following consonant sounds.
We will use them as the first sounds in words to make it easier to pronounce them:

m n ɲ p t k b d g f s ʃ v z ʒ l ʁ

Knowing a little about how French phonemes are formed, or their articulatory characteristics, can help us sound more like native speakers. The goal being not the perfect French accent, but to be easily understood by native speakers of French.

For the most part French consonants are pronounced like their English counterparts–exceptions being / l /, / t / and of course "r", / ʁ /.

For now, let's focus on the vowels, which present very different articulatory characteristics compared to English vowels.

Articulation of vowels, semivowels, and consonants in French is complex. But having a basic understanding of how they are articulated can help us learn to “adjust” our pronunciation to emulate that of native speakers.

If you were a doctor and wanted to inspect your patients' throat or tonsils, what vowel sound would you ask them to say? " a-a-a" of course! It is the most open of the phonemes, in both French and English. It is also a "back" vowel.
